VR & it's usage in the Medical Field

Benefits

One of biggest benefits that this technology can provide is to give surgeons a practice simulation in what to expect or do in a real-time surgery. Sometimes, practice & getting to know the what you're working with will give you confidence when working in the real environment.

"I fear not the man who has practiced 10,000 kicks once, but I fear the man who has practiced one kick 10,000 times." - Bruce Lee

In addition to this benefit, there are also equipments that scan a full 3D body from a patient that they are working with. This way, they can be more accurate on what the problem may be as well as show that patient a more accurate description of what they will be working with on the patient.

Weaknesses

However, with this benefits also comes with some drawbacks. One of them is Functionality. Sometimes, specific software can only be compatible with specific hardware, and if not, it can cause problems, may give inaccurate readings or even give the wrong type of information to the patient & doctor. Additionally, this type of technology is expensive, since most of the tech is still pretty much theoretical and pretty much in prototypes.

Physical Effects

  • Nausea
  • Sim Sickeness
  • Eye Strain

Similar to the effects of VR gaming, too much usage of the technology can lead to eye strain and fatigue. Each effect also depends on the person using it as well as their tolerance to it.
